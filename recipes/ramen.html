Description

Ramen is a Japanese dish consisting of Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a meat or fish-based broth, often flavored with soy sauce or miso, and topped with ingredients such as sliced pork, dried seaweed, green onions, and bamboo shoots. It is typically a hearty and filling meal, and has become popular worldwide as a fast, affordable, and delicious comfort food. There are many regional variations of ramen in Japan, with different broths, noodle types, and toppings, each with its own unique flavor and style.

Ingredients

  • 4 packs of ramen noodles
  • 8 cups of chicken broth (or vegetable broth for a vegetarian option)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp miso paste
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes (optional)
  • 4 boiled eggs, peeled and halved
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 2 sheets of nori (dried seaweed), sliced into thin strips
  • 4 slices of cooked pork belly (optional)
  • 1 cup of bean sprouts (optional)

  1. In a large pot, heat the sesame oil over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and ginger and cook until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add the chicken broth, soy sauce, miso paste, rice vinegar, sugar, and chili flakes (if using) to the pot. Stir well and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and let the broth simmer for about 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. While the broth is simmering, cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  5. Divide the cooked ramen noodles between 4 bowls.
  6. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles in each bowl.
  7. Top each bowl with 2 halves of boiled egg, chopped green onions, sliced nori, pork belly slices (if using), and bean sprouts (if using).
  8. Serve immediately and enjoy!
